TPT TESS PROPOSAL TOOL

Nasa’s Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS) is a space mission designed to search for planets outside of our solar system. Tess will observe the entire sky over two years dividing it into 26 sectors, every 24 degrees by 96 degrees in size. Tess will create a catalog of exoplanets candidates to conduct ground-based follow-up observations to determine the planet’s composition, revealing if planets are rocky like Earth, gas giants (like Jupiter), etc. Further investigation of these exoplanets will be possible using ground- and space-based missions, such as the James Webb Space Telescope, enabling astronomers to analyze their atmospheres. The concept of the TESS Proposal Tool (TPT) is to provide a user-friendly interface for astronomers and researchers to interact with and explore data from the TESS mission.
